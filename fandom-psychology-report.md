# What Are the Psychological Reasons Why People Become Strongly Immersed in Their "Favorite" Celebrities?

---

## 1. Conceptional Background

### Meaning of Fandom Culture

> What fandom is / why it occurs

- Fandom culture: people who passionately like a specific person or field, or the cultural phenomenon itself
- Fandom = fan (fanatic) + -dom (domain/group)

- Fanatic (Latin: fanaticus)
  - Originally meant a person devoted to the church
  - Later changed to mean excessive enthusiasm or deep immersion

- Fandom = a concept that groups people strongly interested in a popular figure or field
  - Developed with the spread of mass media (especially television)
  - Gained cultural influence > "fandom culture" emerged

- Negative aspects (past)
  - Fan conflicts
  - Stalking
  - Cyber attacks
  - Sometimes treated as a subculture

> Today:
- Not necessarily negative
- Recognized as a cultural phenomenon
- Refers to people who actively enjoy stars or popular culture content

---

### Why does fandom form?

- Emotional satisfaction
  - healing
  - emotional bonding
  - sense of belonging
  - active participation (sense of achievement)
  - vicarious satisfaction

- Social communication tool
  - Especially for teenagers
  - builds peer relationships
  - shares culture
  - can develop into social activities (ads, donations)

## 2. Types

### (Idols / Actors)

> Reasons why fans like them

- Emotional identification
  - vicarious achievement
  - feel success of the celebrity as their own

- Vicarious satisfaction
  - projection of ideal self

- Escape from reality
  - stress relief

- Physical attractiveness
  - immediate attraction
  - early stage of interest formation

- Ideal image
  - leads to continuous fan activity

- Emotional bonding
  - intimacy formation
  - attachment / healing

---

### (Sports Athletes)

- Performance / skill
  - competition / results

> Typical process
- performance > interest > personality/charms > appearance becomes attractive

> Difference
- idol fandom = appearance + idealized image
- sports fandom = performance + shared experience

- Appearance is recognized later after emotional interest forms

## 3. Psychological Reasons

- Identification
- parasocial interaction
- sense of belonging

---

### Parasocial Interaction

- One-sided psychological relationship between fan and celebrity
- Not a real relationship, but feels similar

- Examples
  - watching vlogs
  - live broadcasts
  - interviews / shows

> Fans may feel:
- "I feel like I know this person"
- "They feel familiar"
- "I want to support them"

> But no actual personal relationship exists

---

### Characteristics of Parasocial Interaction

- One-sided relationship
  - fan → star
  - star does not know individual fans

- Illusion of intimacy
  - repeated exposure creates familiarity

- Emotional investment
  - feel happy/sad with the celebrity

---

### 1. Idol Fans

- Most commonly studied case

- Reasons
  - vlogs
  - live broadcasts
  - fan communication apps
  - fan meetings

> High content exposure > strong intimacy formation

---

### 2. Actor Fans

- Examples
  - drama / movie characters
  - interviews / variety shows

- Emotional identification with characters
  > Leads to positive feelings toward the actor

---

### 3. Sports Athlete Fans

- Examples
  - game interviews
  - documentaries
  - team content
  - SNS

- Learn about personality and effort
  > Emotional connection forms

> Strength of parasocial interaction differs
(Idols vs Actors vs Athletes)

### Idol Fandom Example
